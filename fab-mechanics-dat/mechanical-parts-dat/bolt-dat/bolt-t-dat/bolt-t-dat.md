An L-bolt (or L-shaped anchor bolt) is a heavy-duty fastener primarily used to anchor structural elements, steel columns, or sill plates to concrete or masonry. The 90-degree bend is embedded in the concrete to prevent the bolt from pulling out under tension.


Key Specifications
* **Sizes:** Commonly available in diameters ranging from M6 to M36 (or 1/2" to 1-1/4") depending on load requirements.
* **Materials:** Manufactured from mild steel, high-tensile steel, or corrosion-resistant 304/316 stainless steel.
* **Finishes:** Available as plain, electro-galvanized, or hot-dipped galvanized (HDG) for outdoor or corrosive environments.



## 4. Primary Applications

* **Structural Steel Columns:** Securing the baseplates of steel structural beams to concrete foundations.
* **Light Poles & Signage:** Anchoring highway sign structures, traffic lights, and street lamp posts.
* **Equipment/Machinery Foundations:** Bolting down heavy industrial manufacturing equipment to prevent vibration-induced shifting.
* **Sill Plate Anchoring:** In residential construction, securing wood sill plates to concrete slab foundations.
