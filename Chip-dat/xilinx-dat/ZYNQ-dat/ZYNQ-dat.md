## Zynq-7000 

The XC7Z020 (part of the AMD/Xilinx Zynq-7000 family) is a highly popular System-on-Chip (SoC) that combines a dual-core ARM Cortex-A9 processor with Artix-7 based programmable logic (FPGA). It is widely used in embedded vision, motor control, and software-defined radio.

### Key Technical Specifications

- **Processing System (PS):** Dual-core ARM Cortex-A9 MPCore up to 667 MHz or 866 MHz (depending on speed grade).
- **Programmable Logic (PL):** 85,000 logic cells, 5.3 Mb block RAM, and 220 DSP slices.
- **Interfaces:** Supports DDR3, DDR2, LPDDR2 memory, and standard peripherals (Gigabit Ethernet, USB 2.0, SD/eMMC, SPI, I2C).
- **Packages Available:** CLG400 (smaller footprint) and CLG484 (more I/O pins).

### Common Development Boards

If you are prototyping or building projects, several ready-to-use platforms feature the XC7Z020:

- **Pynq-Z2:** Highly popular for embedded Python and machine learning applications.
- **Arty Z7:** Excellent for makers and hobbyists.
- **Z-turn Lite:** A compact, low-cost board ideal for industrial evaluations.
- **ALINX AX7020 / AX7021:** Feature-rich boards with multiple video and network interfaces.
